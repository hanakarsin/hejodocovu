The two Kitengela brothers and their two cousins who were allegedly killed on mistaken identity may have met their death in the early evening.

According to Julius Muoki, a fellow biker and friend to Freddie Wanjiru and Victor Wanjiru, Mike George, and Nicholas Musa, the four deceased were not reachable by 7pm on a fateful night.

Muoki was on his way from Ruiru to meet the quartet when he spoke to Mike at around 6pm. He again tried to reach him and the three other friends via phone at 7 pm, but they never returned his calls.

"I would be in this photo because I had travelled from Ruiru where I stay to meet them in Kitengela. 30 minutes before I got to Kitengela, their phones were off. When I reached Mlolongo no one was available. By 7pm they were no picking calls. Let's correct this," he recalled.

Previously, it was reported that the quadruple left their Syokimau home on Sunday, August 8, for a birthday party in Isinya, where they spent the night.

Unconfirmed reports suggested Isinya villagers pounced on them at around 11 pm on suspicion that they were cattle rustlers; the family has dismissed this.

"As they were trying to fix the motorcycle at around 11pm, they were attacked by herdsmen who accused them of plotting to steal their livestock. All of them were killed on the spot," a source said.

Killed by people known to them

A witness alleged that there could have been an altercation between the four and another resident who later came back with other villagers who attacked them.

The villagers attacked them with speakers and other crude weapons, killing them and burning their four high-end motorcycles.

The witness said there were more than 15 private vehicles at the scene. Later, the villagers claimed those killed were livestock thieves.

He added that the four, who were known to the villagers, cried for mercy to no avail.

Former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko has asked God to forgive Kenyans who have ridiculed him online for his motivational posts.

In a powerful message issued on his social media pages on Tuesday, May 4, the controversial politician laughed off claims he had been reduced to blogger and a motivational speaker after his ouster as Nairobi county boss.

"For us, problems are considered as wonderful things because they make us stronger and wiser. At times when we pray those who know nothing about the power of prayer especially when in trouble say we have become or been reduced to motivational speakers or bloggers by the problems we are facing. Lord forgive them for they don't know what they're doing or mean what they say.

The former Nairobi senator stated he had witnessed many miracles in his life and as such, he won't give up on prayer regardless of what people said about him.

The 46-year-old pleaded with God to bless his enemies even if they wished him bad things in life.

"Lord whatever plans you have for us today, please bless all of us including our enemies and the evil-minded, help us all to trust and obey you in Jesus name."

Sonko's impeachment

Sonko was on December 3, 2020, impeached by 88 Nairobi ward representatives over violation of the Constitution, abuse of office and gross misconduct.

Embakasi MCA Michael Ogada who is also the minority leader in the assembly tabled the motion in which he outlined accusations against Sonko.

The Member of County Assembly (MCA) accused the then county chief of sinking the county in unimaginable huge debts besides failing to clear pending bills.

On December 17, the Senate upheld the county assembly's decision to kick out the gold loving politician despite claims of political witch hunt.

The governor who punched his way to national politics was impeached with majority 27 senators voting to send him home while 16 rejected the motion.

Only two senators, Johnson Sakaja (Nairobi) and his Makueni colleague Mutula Kilonzo Junior abstained from the exercise.

Sonko turns to blogging

After his ouster, Sonko said he was excited to go back to his former life before he ventured into politics.

"As I said before, there is life after politics, and I am excited to go back to my life and to be with the ordinary people who have been supporting me all my political life," he said.

Despite denying claims he had been turned into a blogger, the former Makadara lawmaker had in January said he was looking to start a career in journalism after a few weeks out of his government job.

The politician went on social media on Sunday, January 17, alerting his followers that he was delighted to start his new role as a blogger.

"Since I'm now jobless courtesy of Uhuru and his system, I'm happy to be the new blogger in town," he captioned his post.

Boniface Mwangi Sarcastically Praises Kenya Power Employee's Compliment: Id Use My Scent to Li

  • Activist Boniface Mwangi had sought services at the Kenya Power and Lighting Company (KPLC) offices in Mlolongo
  • The lady who served him at the offices could not fail to notice the activist's body scent
  • Mwangi was elated by the compliments, sharing the encounter with his Twitter family

Activist Boniface Mwangi was excited by a Kenya Power employee's compliment that saw him share with netizens.

The activist walked to the utility firm's offices in Mlolongo, Machakos county when his encounter stretched beyond the normal services he had sought at the offices.

The female employee noticed Mwangi's supposed ace scent which she marvelled at, praising him.

Mwangi could not keep calm after the compliments, hailing the staffer for good service as he shared the experience with his Twitter followers.

"I visited Kenya Power Mlolongo and was served by a very pleasant lady. She even said l smell good and l felt nice. I even thought l could just go home and use my scent to light the bulbs. Thank you Mary Wanjiru for serving me with nice words and a smile," said Mwangi on Twitter.

Loving husband

The KPLC lady may not have missed it when she observed that Mwangi is a nice character anyone can associate with.

Recently, the father of three revealed that he kisses his wife and kids every morning and evening, saying those could be the last moments he is sharing with them.

"I kiss my wife, children every morning and evening since it could be the last time I’m seeing them," he wrote on Instagram."We kiss goodbye every morning and welcome home when someone walks through the door. We kiss our children at least twice a day, morning and evening. I love you is an everyday language in our family," he stated.

Mwangi is married to Hellen Njeri and he always held that his marriage is not a perfect one as many may assume.

He once intimated that whenever they have unresolved issues, they sideline their conjugal rights.

"My wife and I agreed no matter how bad our conflicts and issues are, we cuddle every night (no intercourse if we have unresolved issues)" he said.

Mwangi: I was ever broke

Elsewhere, the former politician, who unsuccessfully vied for the Starehe parliamentary seat in 2017, revealed that he was totally broke at some point in life.

His wife Njeri had to take care of the family's bills because he was jobless.

Mwangi shared a throwback video of him and his kids after the 2017 General Election, reminiscing how tough life was for him.

"When l lost the 2017 elections l became a stay at home parent while my wife @njerikan worked and paid the bills," he said.

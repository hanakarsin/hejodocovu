Retired Army General John Koech has died at the Nairobi Hospital after short illness.

Lt. Gen (Rtd.) Koech died on Sunday, November 29, while receiving treatment at the facility.

According to standard, his sons Eric and Robert Koech said the funeral arrangements for the deceased were underway.

Koech will be remembered for serving the country in various spheres including military, politics and agriculture.

The General served as the Army Commandant during President Mwai Kibaki’s reign.

Between 2004 and 2005, the deceased served as a commandant of the National Defence College.

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga and Deputy President William Ruto have led Kenyans in mourning Koech.

The Opposition leader said he had lost a close ally who served in the Kenya army with dedication.

"General John Koech served our country with dedication in the Kenya Army, maintaining the age old tradition of keeping the military out of politics while ensuring the safety and security of the nation from external threats. In retirement, he took his professionalism to politics where we became close allies. I have lost an ally. May God grant his family peace and strength on his passing. Go well my friend." said Raila.

Deputy President William Ruto described him as a progressive and vibrant leader who became a central figure in the transformation of the military.

"Lieutenant-General (rtd) John Koech was a leader of character, a hero, a true patriot and a brave man in uniform who was dedicated to serve and defend our country. Quick-witted, strict but fair-minded, General Koech was a progressive and vibrant leader who became a central figure in the transformation of our military. We will miss his wise counsel, and vision and vigour to empower the people of Ainamoi," said Ruto.

Politics

After he retired from the military, he became the face of the United Democratic Movement (UDM) party supported Railas's presidential quest in 2007.

In 2008, he unsuccessfully sought ODM nomination to contest for the Ainamoi parliamentary seat by-election when the then Member of Parliament David Too was killed.

In 2013, he was also floored by Charles Keter in his quest to serve as the Kericho senator.

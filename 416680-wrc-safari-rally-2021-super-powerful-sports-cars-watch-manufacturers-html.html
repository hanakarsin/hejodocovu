The Safari rally has after a 19-year hiatus returned to Kenyan soil to the excitement of local fans who have thronged to Naivasha to witness the thrilling motor racing event.

Some 58 rally drivers, 34 Kenyan and 24 foreign will take part in this year’s edition that began on Thursday, June 24, and is set to come to a close on Sunday, June 27.

Speed and features of rally cars

Rally cars are very different from normal race cars. This is considering the fact that they need to withstand all sorts of impacts while being driven.

Unlike the smooth, even surface of a race track, a rally stage consists of dips, large rocks, and of course, high jumps that a normal car cannot withstand.

Speeds on the twisty gravel roads average between 80 and 112km/hr, which occasionally bursts over 160km/hr on the straights.

Some of the features of rally cars are a roll cage, upgraded suspension, and better brakes.

To survive the high-speed rough-road pounding, a rally car is usually fitted with a beefier engine while a thick aluminium skid plate prevents sharp rocks from cutting a hole in the vulnerable oil pan.

Powerful sports cars to watch in WRC Safari Rally 2021

TUKO.co.ke looked at the super powerful sports cars to watch in the 2021 WRC Safari Rally and their manufacturers.

1. Toyota Yaris WRC

The Toyota Yaris WRC was designed by Toyota Gazoo Racing WRT to compete in the World Rally Championship.

Frenchman Sébastien Ogier, who has claimed seven WRC titles in the last eight seasons, would be the main driver of the car.

Toyota Yaris WRC costs between KSh 4.2 million and KSh 4.7 million.

2. Hyundai i20 Coupe WRC - Cost $ 20,000

The Hyundai i20 Coupe WRC ( previously known as Hyundai New Generation i20 WRC) was built by Hyundai for use in the World Rally Championship starting in 2017.

It is based on the Hyundai i20 subcompact car and is the successor of the Hyundai i20 WRC used between 2014 and 2015.

In 2020, it won three out of 21 entries it made accumulating 241 points.

Hyundai i20 Coupe WRC rally cost retails at an average price of KSh 2.1 million.

3. Ford Fiesta WRC

The Ford Fiesta WRC is one of the most costly rally cars. It is sold at over KSh 64 million.

It was developed by the M-Sport World Rally Team for use in the World Rally Championship starting in 2017.

The car has never disappointed since making its debut and took five wins in its first season.

4. Ford Fiesta R5 Mk. II

Fiesta R5 Mk. II is an upgraded model of the Ford Fiesta R5 that was launched in 2019 and costs over KSh 20 million.

Its driver Kajetan Kajetanowicz won three consecutive European Rally Championship titles in 2015, 2016 and 2017. It also secured the 2018 European title through Alexey Lukyanuk.

5. Škoda Fabia R5 Evo

Škoda Fabia R5 Evo is an updated version of the Skoda Fabia R5.

The Fabia being sold for at least KSh 23 million is one of the most successful cars in the history of the R5 category, winning over 700 rally competitions all around the world.

6. Volkswagen Polo GTI R5

Prominent Kenyan rally driver Carl Tundo will be driving the Volkswagen Polo GTI R5 in the 2021 WRC Safari Rally.

The car was built by Volkswagen Motorsport and based upon the Volkswagen Polo road car.

It took part in its first competition during the 2018 Catalunya Rally in Spain.

Friday June 25

07:00 hrs: Start of Section 2 at the Naivasha Service Park Friday’s opening leg will see the crews cover the southern shore of Lake Naivasha.

They will tackle the Chui Lodge and Oserian sections - both in the Oserengoni Wildlife Conservancy Estate - as well as Kedong, which at 32.68km is the longest stage of the rally.

After service at noon, all three stages will be repeated in the afternoon to bring the day’s total to 129.78km.

Saturday, June 26

06:55 hrs Start of Section 4 at the Naivasha Service Park

On Saturday, the crews will tackle a total of 132.08km of roads further north around Lake Elmenteita.

They will start with the Elmenteita section in the Delamere Estate, followed by Soysambu and Sleeping Warrior stages.

The stages will be repeated in the afternoon after service.

The “Sleeping Warrior” stage, which is set in the shadow of a hill that resembles a Masai warrior lying down, is set to present a stern test for man and machine.

The roads here are narrow, rocky, and rough to the extreme. They also have twisty corners and short straights, ingredients that evoke the spirit of the old Safari Rally.

Sunday, June 27

06:25 hrs Start of Section 6, Naivasha Service Park

Sunday’s grand finale will span both the north and south sides of Lake Naivasha.

Cars will drive through the forested Loldia area to the north and Hell’s Gate to the south, with the rocky Malewa stage in between.

The Loldia and Hell’s Gate stages will be tackled twice, with the second pass through the latter seeing drivers finish the rally amid spectacular scenery at Fishers Tower (the Wolf Power Stage).

A podium and prize-giving ceremony for the winners at the Naivasha Service Park will mark the end of the 2021 Safari Rally edition.

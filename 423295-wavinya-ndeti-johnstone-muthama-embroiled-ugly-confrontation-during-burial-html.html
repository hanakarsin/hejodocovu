Wavinya Ndeti, Johnstone Muthama Embroiled in Ugly Confrontation during Burial

There was drama during the burial of Mzee James Muthama, the father of Ulbanus Muthama, in Machakos on Friday, August 20.

This was after Transport CAS Wavinya Ndeti and United Democratic Alliance (UDA) chairperson Johnstone Muthama got embroiled in an ugly confrontation that nearly turned physical.

While speaking to mourners, Wavinya asked them to unite behind the One Kenya Alliance, which includes former Vice President and Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka as a co-principal.

Wavinya castigates UDA members

She also chastised Muthama and MPs Vincent Musyoka and Victor Munyaka for supporting for Deputy President William Ruto over Kalonzo in the battle to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta.

“We are going into the 2022 General Election and there are people who are generating a lot of heat...Those criticising OKA’s unity are the enemy of progress in Kenya," stated Wavinya. “We must be united as we all need each other. You cannot claim that you will stand on your own. For us the Kamba, we shall stand with our own,” Wavinya declared.

Senator Kavindu Muthama echoed the position of CAS Wavinya.

Ruto allies retaliate

However, Mwala MP Vincent Musyoka, a Ruto loyalist, retaliated against the CAS, claiming Kalonzo was the underdog.

The CAS was forced to step to the podium to defend Kalonzo as the young MP escalated his venom toward him. Unfortunately, this resulted in a confrontation between the two leaders.

Muthama, the UDA chairperson and former Machakos Senator, nonetheless heightened the tensions at the funeral by accusing the Wiper leaders of worshipping Kalonzo.

He called on the politicians to support each other’s choices and preferences.

Further reports indicated the funeral was momentarily disrupted as the police intervened to calm the agitated and divided followers of the politicians who almost went physical.

Some mourners were reported to have walked out in protest.

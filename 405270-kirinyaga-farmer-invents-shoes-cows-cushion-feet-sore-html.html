A Kirinyanga farmer has narrated how he introduced his cows to wearing shoes after he found out they were limping because of sore feet.

Josephat Kariuki from Kutus said he decided to make the cow shoes from old motorbike tyres and pieces of clothes to cushion his animals from sore feet.

Speaking to a local TV station, Kariuki said getting the cows to wear the shoes at first was not an easy feat as they kept resisting the footwear by stomping their feet on the ground.

Fortunately, with time, his cows started getting accustomed to their special footwear and would ever offer their feet without resistance when Kariuki was fitting them in.

Cow-shoe business

It is from this idea that he decided to make shoes for other cows in his hometown and gradually, his venture started making him money.

He said he sells the cow shoes at KSh 400 for a quartet adding he has many clients as the footwear has proven worthwhile in protecting hooves.

"The shoes make the cow more stable," Kariuki said.

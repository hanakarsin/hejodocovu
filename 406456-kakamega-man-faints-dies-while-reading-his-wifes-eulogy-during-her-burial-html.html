Residents of Mukhula village in Navakholo, Kakamega county were left in shock after a double tragedy where a man fainted and died while eulogising his wife.

The man, Daniel Tauni, reportedly took to the podium to read his wife Jane Koikoi's eulogy when he was overwhelmed and suddenly collapsed.

The 55-year-old was immediately rushed to Navakholo Sub-County Hospital after the Friday, March 19, incident where he was pronounced dead on arrival.

Tauni was returned home and laid to rest concurrently with his wife.

Koikoi reportedly succumbed to Tuberculosis complications but the cause of his husband's death was unknown as his family members said he had no history of heart problems.

The family of the deceased appealed to wellwishers to help their two children as they were expected to sit for the forthcoming national exams.

In a separate story, a police officer with the Ghana Police Service, Chief Superintendent Michael Vieira, has reportedly passed away after recently losing his wife.

Chief Superintendent Vieira was making arrangements to give his wife a befitting burial before his unexpected demise.

TUKO.co.ke gathered he passed away the dawn of Saturday, February 20, after battling for his life due to ill health.

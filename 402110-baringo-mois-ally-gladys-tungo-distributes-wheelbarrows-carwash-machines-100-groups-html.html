Baringo Woman Representative Gladys Tungo, a close ally of Senator Gideon Moi, on Friday, February 5, distributed wheelbarrows and carwash machines to over 100 groups raising eyebrows on whether she had joined the hustler nation.

The Kenya African National Union (KANU) lawmaker also handed over a cheque worth KSh 7.9 million to 41 youth groups and 20 women groups under national affirmation action funds which falls under her office.

In a Facebook post, the legislator said the move would go along way in boosting the livelihoods of beneficiaries as a way of eradicating poverty in the community and also addressing the rampant cases of unemployment.

"Also handed over water tanks, ballast breaking tools and fishing nets to over 100 groups to empower their daily business. I encourage more youth and women to apply for Affirmative funds to improve their lives," the woman rep said.

Her post sparked excitement from some Tanga Tanga supporters who thought she had joined the wheelbarrow movement associated with Deputy President William Ruto who is Moi's rival in the Rift Valley region.

But in a quick rejoinder however, Tungo dismissed claims she had joined Ruto's faction saying she was just empowering youth and women in the process.

In a video shared on a Facebook page dubbed North Rift News, the woman rep insisted she was firmly in the independence party and would continue supporting it.

"I know my move to distribute several items including wheelbarrows has made some people to think I have defected to the hustler nation but that is not the case. I am still in KANU and we will use wheelbarrows for our daily activities but and that doesn't mean we are enhancing wheelbarrow politics," she said.

Deputy President William Ruto has been charming the youth with his charity work and donations including wheelbarrows allegedly aimed at empowering them economically.

The DP recently dismissed critics castigating his wheelbarrow donations saying the initiative was similar to President Uhuru Kenyatta's Kazi Mtaani.

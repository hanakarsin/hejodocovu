Guess Who Didn't Get a Rose? 'The Bachelor' Season 28 Elimination Recap

Discover the latest updates on the contestants who went home on Season 28 of 'The Bachelor' and those still competing for Joey Graziadei's heart.

By Allison DeGrushe

Feb. 26 2024, Published 10:01 p.m. ET

Spoiler alert: This article contains major spoilers for Season 28 of The Bachelor.

Get ready to toss those coveted roses in the air and pop open the wine bottles because Season 28 of The Bachelor is here to steal your heart! Our charming leading man, Joey Graziadei, is already stealing hearts left and right, creating a love-filled atmosphere for both his contestants and the viewers enjoying the show from the comfort of their homes.

While Joey's journey is already an entertaining ride, there's one thing that remains constant season after season: Only one lucky lady gets to ride off into the sunset with the leading heartthrob. So, brace yourself for the whirlwind of petty drama that's about to unfold as more contestants are sent packing in each episode.

Curious to know who went home on The Bachelor Season 28? Dive into the juicy details below!

*SPOILERS FOR WEEK 7 BELOW*

Who went home on Season 28 of 'The Bachelor'?

Week 7 — Feb. 26, 2024

After taking extra time to think things over, Joey abruptly reaches a decision. Consequently, he decides to forgo the cocktail party and proceeds directly to the rose ceremony, where he sends Kelsey Toussant and Jenn Tran home.

Week 6 — Feb. 19, 2024

During the night portion of the group date, Joey eliminates Jess Edwards. Later, prior to the rose ceremony, Lexi Young self-eliminates, citing misalignment in her and Joey's timelines for marriage and children.

Then, Joey says goodbye to Katelyn DeBacker and Lea Cayanan at the rose ceremony, bringing closure to this chapter of over-the-top villains and unnecessary drama once and for all!

Week 5 — Feb. 13, 2024

The drama spills into the second episode of The Bachelor's two-night event, but fortunately, all parties involved live to see another day! However, at the rose ceremony, Joey sends home Madina Alam and Autumn Waggoner.

Week 4 — Feb. 12-13, 2024

Joey thought jetting off overseas with his remaining ladies would be smooth sailing, but his dreams of a drama-free trip take an unexpected detour. Undeterred, he arranges a 2-on-1 date to address tensions between Maria Georgas and Sydney Gordon. After thoughtful consideration, Joey follows his heart and sends Sydney home.

During the rose ceremony, Joey eliminates Edwina Dorbor and Allison Hollinger.

Week 3 — Feb. 5, 2024

It's like clockwork at a Bachelor pool party — drama steals the spotlight, hogging precious time from the gals eager to chat with Joey and amp up their connection. Among those frustrated by the petty drama is Evalin Clark, and sadly, her fears come true: She doesn't receive a rose during the third rose ceremony.

Joey eliminates two more ladies during the rose ceremony: Chrissa Perez and Starr Skyler.

Week 2 — Jan. 29, 2024

Following a challenging experience on the "wedding reception" group date, Lauren Hollinger is determined to make a comeback at the cocktail party. Yet, as Joey chats with other women, her frustration mounts. By the night's conclusion, Lauren's desire to leave overwhelms her, leading her to self-eliminate before the second rose ceremony.

Speaking of the rose ceremony, Joey eliminates Taylor Weins, Erika Cardenas, and Marlena Haddad.

Week 1 — Jan. 22, 2024

In a bold move reminiscent of his Bachelor predecessor, Zach Shallcross, leading man Joey Graziadei decides to give 10 lovely ladies an early exit. With that said, let's bid farewell to Chandler Dewgard, Kayla Rodgers, Kyra Brusch, Lanie Latsios, Natalie Crepeau, Sam Hale, Samantha Washington, Sandra Rabadi, Talyah Jackson, and Zoe Antona.

Tune in every Monday at 8 p.m. EST on ABC to see who goes home on The Bachelor!

Kikuyu Member of Parliament Kimani Ichung'wah has shown off a fleet of wheelbarrows he owns at his home.

The Tanga Tanga allied lawmaker has associated himself with the revamped United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party whose symbol is the wheelbarrow and slogan is "kazi ni kazi."

The vocal Tanga Tanga lawmaker was dressed in jeans and a white top and was seen carrying soil at an undisclosed location.

"That guy who said wheelbarrows are useless...Can he bring his V8 vehicle so I can use it to carry soil? Today I have been hustling hard," she wrote on her Facebook page.

Deputy President William Ruto has been charming the youth with his charity work and donations allegedly aimed at empowering them economically.

The wheelbarrow has been one of the items on his donations list to the chagrin of former prime minister Raila Odinga and other leaders who have insisted the youths do not need them in the 21st century.

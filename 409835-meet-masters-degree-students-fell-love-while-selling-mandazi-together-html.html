Shukrani Mwendia and her husband were united by their love for business and their common desire to venture into self-employment.

Shurkrani was taking business studies while her husband John Munyi was a production student when they first met at Chuka University.

Speaking to The Standard, Munyi said on the particular day they met, Shukrani was not feeling so well and so he took care of her and after that incident, they became friends.

"I came to know she is good in business and so we started cooking mandazi and selling and shortly after, we fell in love," he said.

However, their mandazi business did not survive after the ban on polythene bags and they thus decided to do market research together to see what other business they would undertake together.

It is through this their sugarcane juice shop was opened and the skills and knowledge that they both acquired came in handy in their new venture.

"My husband is doing his masters in hotel management and so he is very good in production. So he stays in the shop and makes the juice for me while I market it around (I Embu) because I did marketing," she said.

Munyi juggles doing his research for his masters while also managing their shop and producing the juice.

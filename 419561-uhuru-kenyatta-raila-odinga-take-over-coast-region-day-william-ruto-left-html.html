President Uhuru Kenyatta and ODM party leader Raila Odinga are in the coastal region for a tour.

Uhuru, Odinga in Coast

The president is in the coast region for a three-day visit during which he will commission his legacy projects, whereas Raila is there for his political campaigns.

The head of state landed there on Tuesday, July 21, while Raila arrived on Saturday, July 18.

Their visit stirred political interest given the heightened campaigns in the region led by Deputy President William Ruto.

Uhuru is set to meet with political leaders from six counties in the coast region on Thursday on July 21, for a briefing on the progress of his legacy projects.

However, it was not immediately clear whether the president will team up with ODM leader Raila Odinga who is also touring the region.

In June, Uhuru instructed those implementing his landmark Coast projects to ensure they are on schedule for completion before leaving office in August 2022.

According to Mvita MP Abdulswamad Nassir, the president's visit was planned a long time ago.

“We shall meet him as the Coast Parliamentary Group on Thursday. It is a coincidence that our party leader Odinga is also here,” Nassir said.

Although it’s an official trip, it has stirred political excitement since it has come immediately after the DP’s campaigns in the region and coincided with Raila’s tour.

Uhuru's projects

The president has already met the governors, Ali Hassan Joho (Mombasa), Amason Kingi (Kilifi) and Salim Mvurya (Kwale) for talks.

Since 2013, the Jubilee administration has implemented projects in the coast region worth KSh 1.3 trillion.

The government has reportedly spent more than KSh 240 billion in Mombasa and has earmarked another close to KSh 80 billion in the 2021/2022 financial year.

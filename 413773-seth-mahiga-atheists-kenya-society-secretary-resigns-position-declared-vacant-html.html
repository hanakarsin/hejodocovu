Atheists in Kenya Society (AIK) has suffered a blow following the resignation of their secretary Seth Mahiga after serving for one and half years.

Mahiga stepped down from the position citing he had found Jesus Christ and is no longer interested in promoting atheism in Kenya.

In a statement on Saturday, May 29, the society's president Harrison Mumia eulogised him as a dedicated leader, wishing him the best in his newfound relationship with Jesus Christ.

"We wish Seth all the best in his newfound relationship with Jesus Christ, we thank him for having served the society with dedication over the last one and half years," read the statement in part.

Consequently, the non-believers announced a vacancy for the position inviting interested Kenyan atheists to apply.

Those who wish to apply for the post to join the society's executive committee can send their curriculum vitae to info@atheistsinkenya.org.

Reactions

Mahiga's resignation elicited mixed reactions on social media with the majority of the tweeps congratulating him for the bold decision.

Some urged Mumia to follow suit and see the light. Here are some comments sampled by TUKO.co.ke:

Former Chief Justice Willy Mutunga said: "President. If it is of any consolation, besides your LOL, Article 32(1) of our Constitution provides: Every person has the right to freedom of conscience, religion, thought, belief and opinion. This provision is not a laughing matter."

In a separate story, the atheists' society offered to cover comedienne Stella Jemutai's rent arrears following her recent accusations against her baby daddy Professor Hamo.

In a statement, the society's president Harrison Mumia asked the entertainer to get in touch with them so they could offer a helping hand.

The society also noted single mothers were heavily affected and urged the government to get a stimulus bursary working for them.

"We ask the government to set aside a stimulus grant for single mothers in Kenya. This fund should support single moms struggling with money, health, child care and illness," the statement read.

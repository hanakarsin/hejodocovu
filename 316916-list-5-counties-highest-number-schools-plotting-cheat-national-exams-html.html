List of 5 counties with highest number of schools plotting to cheat in national exams

May 28, 2024
Post

- At least 39 counties are on KNEC's radar over shocking cheating claims

- The cheating racket has teachers, students and owners of suspect schools

- A total 315 schools have been flagged for potential examination malpractices

The Ministry of Education has unearthed an exam cheating racket that mainly includes its officials, teachers, students and moneyed school owners across the country.

In a shocking revelation, the Kenya National Examination Council (KNEC) placed a total 315 schools on its radar over cheating claims in the forthcoming national examinations.

A report filed by Standard on Wednesday, October 2, further confirmed at least 39 counties are under surveillance for the same even as the council counts days to kick off of the 2019 exams.

Out of the suspected learning institutions, 190 primary schools and 125 secondary schools have been earmarked and are believed to be plotting to commit various examination malpractices.

Here is a list of five counties with highest number of schools (both primary and secondary) on KNEC's radar:

1. Garissa - 63

2. Wajir - 50

3. West Pokot - 22

4. Mandera - 21

5. Nairobi - 18

2. Narok - 1

3. Embu - 1

4. Murang'a - 1

5. Nyeri - 1

6. Kericho - 1

7. Kwale - 1

8. Kirinyaga - 1

9. Kitui - 2

10. Uasin Gishu -2

The Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) candidates will begin their exams on October 28, 2019, and finish their last paper on October 31.

Those in Form Four will write their first Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education exams on November 4, 2019, and the last paper will be done on November 27.

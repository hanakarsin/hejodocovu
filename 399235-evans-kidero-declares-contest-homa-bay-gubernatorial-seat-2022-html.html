The former Nairobi governor Evans Kidero has returned to his home county of Homa Bay to seek the gubernatorial seat in the 2022 General Election.

Kidero claimed he had been approached by area residents who implored on him to vie for the seat to offer an alternative leadership.

Speaking after grassroots engagement in Rangwe constituency on Friday, January 8, the politician told the gathering he was the change agent the South Nyanza county had been looking for.

"It won't be about people and it won't be about events it will be about ideas. People in Homa Bay want change and that is why some other politicians who also wanted to vie for the same seat have stepped down and we have been able to sit because all we want is to develop this region," he said.

Kidero said Homa Bay County has several resources which can be used to transform the living standards of local residents.

"People go up to even three days here without electric power and yet we have steam at Homa hill which can be converted to energy, we also have sweet potatoes here at Kabondo Kasipul which can also be resourceful for our people," he noted.

At the same time, he affirmed that he was still an active member of ODM party on whose platform he would vie as.

"I am a very active member of ODM party and I will vie for the seat using the party," the former governor disclosed.

The former Mumias Sugar Company managing director and the first Nairobi governor lost his bid to recapture the seat in 2017.

Corruption allegations

Though the governor had showed intentions to run for the top county office, he will be going into the gubernatorial race with a cloud of corruption allegations hovering over his head.

TUKO.co.ke had reported Kidero was arrested in 2018 and charged with abuse of office and fraud of KSh 213 million when he served as Nairobi governor, charges which he denied and was released on a KSh 2 million cash bail.

According to the charge sheet, Kidero was accused of conspiring with former City Hall finance boss Maurice Ochieng’ Okere to swindle City Hall.

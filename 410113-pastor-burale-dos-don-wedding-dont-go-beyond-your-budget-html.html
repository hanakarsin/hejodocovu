Pastor Burale on Dos and Don'ts of Wedding: &quot;Don't Go Beyond Your Budget&quot;

- A wedding is a special occasion and one should stick to their budget or end up in debts

- Stylish pastor Robert Burale has warned couples against involving people to fundraise for their weddings

- According to Pastor Burale, who hosts Oh! Men Show on Switch TV, couples should hold a wedding based on their financial capabilities

- Many couples take loans to hold big weddings to make guests happy without thinking about the future

It is always a dream of every woman to walk down the aisle one day with her prince charming. But only a few are lucky to have a wedding.

Weddings are expensive nowadays, and many prefer to go to Attorney General's office to officiate their union.

But for some, they would rather invite people to contribute towards their wedding to have a mega event on this special day, which comes once in a lifetime. Others even get loans for their wedding and are left in debts after the occasion.

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, relationship coach Pastor Robert Burale advised couples not to depend on other people to fundraise for their weddings. He said couples should stick to their budget.

"One has to consider their budget and not go beyond it. Just do a wedding based on your capability, financial elasticities because there is no point in getting stressed after the wedding," he said.

In most cases, where couples go beyond their budget, they end up depressed if they don't recover the money from guests. Some even divorce because they are no longer happy in their marriage.

In a related article, TUKO.co.ke shared about a Molo couple who left their guests disappointed after holding their church wedding without a reception to entertain them.

On Saturday, April 3, the unusual union highlighted on NTV saw Mary Wairimu and Amos Mburu joined in holy matrimony with only 30 guests in attendance.
